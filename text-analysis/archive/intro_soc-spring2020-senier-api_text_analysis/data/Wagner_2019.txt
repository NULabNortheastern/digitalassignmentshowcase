Major league players will now be tested for opioids and cocaine, but violators will be evaluated for treatment programs rather than immediately punished.SAN DIEGO — After a season that saw the opioid-overdose death of a 27-year-old pitcher, Major League Baseball and the players’ union on Thursday announced that players will be tested for opioids and cocaine starting in spring training under an updated drug policy. Major league players will now be tested for opioids and cocaine, but violators will be evaluated for treatment programs rather than immediately punished.
