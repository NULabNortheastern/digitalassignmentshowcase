Nonprescription drugs are convenient, but they can be overused or used for the wrong purpose.Four out of five American adults commonly take over-the-counter medications, most often to treat ailments like aches and pains, coughs and colds, fever, allergies, skin disorders, and heartburn and other digestive problems. The reasons are easy to understand. O.T.C.s are convenient, readily available in groceries and big box stores as well as pharmacies, and they are less expensive than going to the doctor and perhaps paying for a costly prescription.Nonprescription drugs are convenient, but they can be overused or used for the wrong purpose.
