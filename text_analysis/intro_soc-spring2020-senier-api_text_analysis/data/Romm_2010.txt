An expansive mix of medical reportage, history and memoir explores our relationship to pain.A simple understanding of pain would be this: It’s a biological response to injury or disease. Treat the cause and the pain abates. The body, after all, is a relatively efficient machine. But what happens when the wound won’t heal? And what if pain doesn’t stem from injury or disease, but is itself the problem?An expansive mix of medical reportage, history and memoir explores our relationship to pain.
