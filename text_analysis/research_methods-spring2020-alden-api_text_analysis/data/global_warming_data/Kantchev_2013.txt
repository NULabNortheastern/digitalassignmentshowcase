By midcentury, Britain could become one of the world’s big wine producers, as warmer weather moves the limits of viticulture ever farther north.DORKING, England — For more than a decade, Matthieu Elzinga ran his own vineyard in the western Loire Valley of France. But this year, just as he was gaining an international reputation for his dry and crisp Muscadets, Mr. Elzinga sold the vineyard and moved to an emerging wine region: the south of England.By midcentury, Britain could become one of the world’s big wine producers, as warmer weather moves the limits of viticulture ever farther north.
