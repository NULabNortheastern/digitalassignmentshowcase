He isn’t the first president to understand the power of television. But he is alone in not grasping its limits.As with most politicians, Franklin D. Roosevelt loved attention and approval in equal measure. Once, after watching himself in a newsreel, he remarked, “That was the Garbo in me.” On meeting Orson Welles, the president said, “You know, Orson, you and I are the two best actors in America!”He isn’t the first president to understand the power of television.
